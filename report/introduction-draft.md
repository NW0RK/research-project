# Introduction Draft

## Background

Internships are an important part of undergraduate education because they help students connect academic knowledge with professional practice. Through internships, students can gain work experience, explore career interests, build professional networks, and improve their confidence in applying for future employment.

For universities, internship support services can play a valuable role in helping students access opportunities and prepare for the application process. These services may include sharing internship announcements, organizing employer events, reviewing CVs, offering interview preparation, and advising students about application expectations.

## Problem Statement

Although internship support services may be available, their effectiveness depends on whether students are aware of them, use them, and find them useful. If students do not know where to find internship information or feel that support is limited, they may be less prepared to search for and apply to internships.

At KIU, it is useful to examine student perceptions of internship support services in order to identify strengths, weaknesses, and opportunities for improvement.

## Research Question

This study is guided by the following research question:

> How effective do KIU undergraduate students perceive KIU's internship support services to be in helping them find, prepare for, and apply to internships?

## Hypotheses

H0: There is no significant difference in perceived internship readiness between students who have used KIU internship support services and students who have not.

H1: Students who have used KIU internship support services report significantly higher perceived internship readiness than students who have not.

## Significance of the Study

The findings may help the university understand how students experience internship support services and which improvements would be most useful. The study may also help students and staff identify ways to strengthen communication, practical preparation, and employer engagement.
