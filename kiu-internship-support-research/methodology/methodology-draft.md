# Methodology Draft

## Research Design

This study uses a quantitative, cross-sectional survey design with a small number of open-ended questions. The survey is designed to collect undergraduate students' perceptions of KIU internship support services at one point in time.

The quantitative items measure awareness, usage, satisfaction, perceived usefulness, and internship readiness. The open-ended questions collect student views about weaknesses and possible improvements.

## Population

The target population is KIU undergraduate students. The study is especially interested in students who may be searching for internships, preparing to apply, or already have internship experience.

## Sampling Method

The study will use convenience sampling because the questionnaire will be distributed through accessible student channels, such as class groups, student networks, and university communication platforms.

Example wording:

> A convenience sampling approach was used because the project was conducted within a limited course timeline and aimed to collect responses from accessible KIU undergraduate students.

## Data Collection Method

Data will be collected using a Google Forms questionnaire. The form will include:

- Demographic and academic background questions
- Internship search and experience questions
- Likert-scale items about internship support services
- Internship readiness items
- Open-ended questions about weaknesses and improvements

The questionnaire should begin with a short consent statement explaining the purpose of the study, voluntary participation, anonymity, and estimated completion time.

## Variables

The main grouping variable is whether the student has used KIU internship support services. The main outcome variable is perceived internship readiness, calculated from confidence and readiness-related items.

Service evaluation variables include information availability, communication quality, application support, CV support, interview support, industry connections, accessibility, and overall satisfaction.

## Data Analysis

The analysis will include descriptive statistics and one inferential test.

Descriptive analysis will report:

- Frequencies and percentages for categorical variables
- Mean scores for satisfaction and readiness items
- Common weaknesses and improvement suggestions

Inferential analysis will compare the mean readiness score of students who used support services with students who did not. If both groups have enough valid responses, an independent samples t-test will be used.

## Ethical Considerations

Participation will be voluntary. The survey should not collect names, student ID numbers, or other direct identifiers. Participants should be informed that their responses will be used only for academic research in the course project. Data should be stored securely and reported in aggregate form.

## Limitations

Possible limitations include convenience sampling, small sample size, self-reported perceptions, and limited generalizability. The study measures perceived effectiveness rather than direct internship placement outcomes.
