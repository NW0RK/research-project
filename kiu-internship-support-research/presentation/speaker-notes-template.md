# Speaker Notes Template

Use this file to prepare short speaking notes for each slide. Notes should sound natural and should not repeat every word on the slide.

## Slide 1: Title

Speaker:

Notes:

- Welcome the audience.
- State the project title.
- Introduce team members.

## Slide 2: Problem and Motivation

Speaker:

Notes:

- Explain why internships matter for undergraduate students.
- Mention the need for support in finding and applying to internships.

## Slide 3: Research Question

Speaker:

Notes:

- Read the research question clearly.
- Explain that the study focuses on student perceptions of effectiveness.

## Slide 4: Hypotheses

Speaker:

Notes:

- Explain the difference between H0 and H1.
- Connect the hypotheses to readiness scores.

## Slide 5: Literature Review Themes

Speaker:

Notes:

- Summarize the most relevant literature themes.
- Mention how the literature informed the survey design.

## Slide 6: Methodology

Speaker:

Notes:

- Identify the design, sample, and data collection method.
- Mention Google Forms and convenience sampling.

## Slide 7: Survey Design

Speaker:

Notes:

- Explain the main sections of the questionnaire.
- Mention the 1-5 Likert scale.

## Slide 8: Sample

Speaker:

Notes:

- Report number of responses.
- Highlight year and school distribution.

## Slide 9: Descriptive Results

Speaker:

Notes:

- Present the most important descriptive findings.
- Avoid reading every number.

## Slide 10: Inferential Results

Speaker:

Notes:

- Explain the readiness comparison.
- Report t-test result and conclusion.

## Slide 11: Open-Ended Findings

Speaker:

Notes:

- Summarize common student suggestions.
- Connect comments to practical improvements.

## Slide 12: Discussion

Speaker:

Notes:

- Interpret what the findings mean.
- Link the results back to the literature.

## Slide 13: Limitations

Speaker:

Notes:

- Be honest and concise.
- Explain why limitations do not make the project useless.

## Slide 14: Recommendations

Speaker:

Notes:

- Present recommendations as realistic actions.
- Prioritize the most evidence-based suggestions.

## Slide 15: Conclusion

Speaker:

Notes:

- Answer the research question.
- End with a clear final message.

## Slide 16: Q&A

Speaker:

Notes:

- Invite questions.
- Keep answers short and evidence-based.
