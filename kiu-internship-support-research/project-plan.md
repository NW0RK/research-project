# Project Plan

## Project Title

Evaluating the Effectiveness of KIU's Internship Support Services for Undergraduate Students

## Purpose

This project examines how KIU undergraduate students perceive internship support services offered by the university. The goal is to understand whether students are aware of the services, whether they use them, how satisfied they are, and whether service usage is associated with stronger perceived internship readiness.

## Deliverables

- Research question and hypotheses
- Literature review and source matrix
- Methodology section
- Google Forms questionnaire
- Cleaned survey dataset
- Descriptive and inferential analysis
- Final academic report
- Team presentation
- Appendices, including consent form and questionnaire

## Suggested Timeline

| Week | Task | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | Confirm topic, research question, and hypotheses | Approved research scope |
| Week 1 | Search literature and build source matrix | Completed source matrix draft |
| Week 2 | Draft methodology and survey instrument | Google Forms questionnaire |
| Week 2 | Pilot test survey with 3-5 students | Revised questionnaire |
| Week 3 | Collect responses | CSV export from Google Forms |
| Week 3 | Clean and analyze data | Summary tables and charts |
| Week 4 | Draft report sections | Full report draft |
| Week 4 | Build and rehearse presentation | Final slide script |

## Team Roles

| Role | Responsibilities |
|---|---|
| Project coordinator | Keeps schedule, checks deliverables, submits final files |
| Literature lead | Manages search strategy, source matrix, and literature review |
| Methodology lead | Drafts design, sampling, ethics, and questionnaire sections |
| Data lead | Exports data, cleans dataset, runs Python analysis |
| Report editor | Combines sections, checks academic tone, formats references |
| Presentation lead | Builds slide flow and coordinates rehearsal |

## Key Decisions To Make Early

- Exact population: all KIU undergraduates or selected schools/year levels
- Minimum target sample size
- Survey distribution channels
- Deadline for closing Google Forms
- Citation style required by the instructor
- Whether to report school-level comparisons or only overall results

## Success Criteria

The project is successful if the team can clearly answer the research question using survey evidence, present descriptive results, test the hypothesis about internship readiness, and provide practical recommendations for improving internship support services at KIU.

## Risk Management

| Risk | Possible Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Low response rate | Weak analysis and limited conclusions | Share reminders and use multiple student channels |
| Unclear survey items | Low-quality data | Pilot test before distribution |
| Missing data | Harder statistical comparison | Keep essential questions required and document exclusions |
| Biased sample | Results may not represent all students | Describe sampling limitations honestly |
| Late analysis | Weak report and presentation | Run analysis first on dummy data, then replace with real data |
