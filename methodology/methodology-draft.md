# Methodology Draft

## Research Design

This study uses a quantitative, cross-sectional survey design with a small number of open-ended questions. The survey collects undergraduate students' perceptions of KIU internship support services at one point in time.

The quantitative items measure awareness, usage, satisfaction among support users, perceived usefulness, reasons for non-use, improvement priorities, and internship readiness. The open-ended questions collect student views about weaknesses and possible improvements.

## Population

The target population is KIU undergraduate students. The study is relevant to students in different years of study because internship awareness and readiness can develop before students actually apply for internships.

## Sampling Method

The study will use convenience sampling because the questionnaire will be distributed through accessible student channels, such as class groups, student networks, and university communication platforms. The team should aim for at least 50 valid responses if possible. A sample of 80 or more responses would make subgroup descriptions more useful, while 30 responses should be treated as the minimum practical threshold for descriptive reporting.

Suggested report wording:

> A convenience sampling approach was used because the project was conducted within a limited course timeline and aimed to collect responses from accessible KIU undergraduate students. Therefore, the findings should be interpreted as perceptions of the surveyed students rather than as statistically representative evidence for all KIU undergraduates.

## Data Collection Method

Data will be collected using a Google Forms questionnaire. The form will include:

- Academic background questions
- Internship search and completion questions
- Awareness and usage items
- Branching questions for support users and non-users
- Likert-scale service-evaluation items for support users
- Internship readiness items for all consenting respondents
- Improvement priority and open-ended recommendation questions

The questionnaire begins with a consent statement explaining the purpose of the study, voluntary participation, anonymity, and estimated completion time. If a respondent does not consent, Google Forms should send the respondent to a final thank-you section and submit the form.

## Variables

The main grouping variable is whether the student has used KIU internship support services. The main outcome variable is perceived internship readiness, calculated as the mean of four neutral readiness items: confidence finding internship opportunities, confidence preparing a CV or resume, confidence preparing for interviews, and understanding employer expectations.

Service evaluation variables include information availability, communication quality, application support, CV support, interview support, industry connections, accessibility, and overall satisfaction. These items are answered only by students who have used support services. The item `support_readiness_impact` is analyzed separately because it directly asks support users whether KIU support improved their readiness.

## Data Analysis

The analysis will include descriptive statistics, reliability checks where sample size permits, and one inferential comparison.

Descriptive analysis will report:

- Frequencies and percentages for categorical variables
- Mean scores for awareness, service evaluation, readiness, and support impact
- Missing-value counts for key variables
- Common weakness and improvement themes from open-ended responses

Inferential analysis will compare the mean readiness score of students who used support services with students who did not. If both groups have at least two valid readiness scores, a Welch independent samples t-test will be used. The report should include group means, mean difference, p-value, 95% confidence interval, and Cohen's d. The result should be described as an association, not as causal proof.

## Ethical Considerations

Participation will be voluntary. The survey should not collect names, student ID numbers, emails, phone numbers, or other direct identifiers. Participants should be informed that their responses will be used only for academic research in the course project. Data should be stored securely and reported in aggregate form.

## Limitations

Possible limitations include convenience sampling, small sample size, self-reported perceptions, possible nonresponse bias, and limited generalizability. The study measures perceived effectiveness and readiness rather than direct internship placement outcomes.
