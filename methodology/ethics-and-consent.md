# Ethics and Consent

## Ethical Principles

This study should follow basic ethical principles for student research:

- Participation must be voluntary.
- Participants should understand the purpose of the study.
- Participants should be able to stop at any time before submitting the form.
- The survey should avoid collecting direct personal identifiers.
- Results should be reported in aggregate form.
- Raw data should be shared only within the research team and instructor if required.

## Consent Text for Google Forms

Use or adapt the following text at the beginning of the questionnaire:

> We are conducting a student research project for the Basic Research Methods and Tools course. The purpose of this survey is to understand how KIU undergraduate students perceive KIU's internship support services. The survey should take approximately 5-7 minutes. Participation is voluntary, and you may stop before submitting the form. Please do not include your name, student ID, phone number, or other identifying information in open-ended responses. Your answers will be used only for academic purposes and reported in summary form.

## Consent Question

Question:

> Do you agree to participate in this survey?

Response options:

- Yes, I agree to participate.
- No, I do not agree to participate.

If possible, configure the form so students who select "No" do not continue to the main questionnaire.

## Data Protection Plan

- Store the Google Forms response sheet in a team-controlled Google Drive folder.
- Download the CSV only when needed for analysis.
- Do not upload identifiable data to public repositories.
- If the final repository is public, include only dummy data or anonymized data.
- Remove accidental names or contact details from open-ended responses before reporting.

## Reporting Ethics

When writing results:

- Do not quote a response if it could identify a student.
- Combine similar open-ended comments into themes.
- Report small subgroups carefully.
- Avoid blaming individuals or offices; focus on service improvement.
