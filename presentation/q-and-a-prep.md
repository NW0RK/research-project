# Presentation Q&A Prep

Use these short answers to prepare for likely instructor or audience questions.

## Why did you use a survey?

The research question focuses on student perceptions, so a survey is appropriate because it can collect comparable responses from many students in a short time. The Likert-scale items allow descriptive statistics and group comparison, while the open-ended questions capture improvement ideas.

## Why did you use convenience sampling?

The project was completed within a course timeline, so the team distributed the form through accessible student channels. This is practical, but it means the findings should be interpreted as the perceptions of surveyed students rather than as fully representative evidence for all KIU undergraduates.

## How did you measure internship readiness?

Readiness was measured with four neutral items: confidence finding internship opportunities, confidence preparing a CV or resume, confidence preparing for interviews, and understanding employer expectations. The item about whether KIU support improved readiness was analyzed separately because it applies only to students who used support services.

## Why did you use a Welch t-test?

The main hypothesis compares mean readiness scores between students who used internship support services and students who did not. Welch's t-test is appropriate because it compares two independent groups and does not require the two groups to have equal variances.

## Can you say support services caused higher readiness?

No. Because the study is cross-sectional and uses convenience sampling, the result should be described as an association. Students who are already more motivated may also be more likely to use support services.

## What is the biggest limitation?

The biggest limitation is likely convenience sampling, especially if the final response count is small. Self-reported perceptions and lack of direct internship outcome data are also important limitations.

## What should KIU improve first?

Use the actual top improvement priority from the survey. If the dummy pattern is similar to the real data, the strongest first steps are earlier communication, more employer and alumni events, and a central internship information platform.

## Why include open-ended responses?

The open-ended responses help explain the numerical results. For example, a low score for industry connections becomes more useful if students also describe wanting more employer events, alumni examples, or field-specific partners.
